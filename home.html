About Us

The History Club was founded in 1976 with the intent of enhancing and popularizing historical knowledge within the school with the aim of observing the past to secure and improve our own future. For this the History Club organizes seminars, workshops and quizzes across the year for students from across the country.

Royal College History Awareness Initiative

The Royal College History Awareness Initiative, hosted by the History Club in September and October, engaged students from grades 6 to 11. Conducting sessions in 25+ classrooms, committee members utilized various teaching methods to explore Royal College's history, traditions, and milestones. By delving into archives, consulting with Prefects and Old Boys, the initiative crafted a narrative that instilled pride and connection within the school community, leaving a lasting impact on the appreciation of its historical heritage.

Chronology'23

The Royal College History Club concluded "Chronology'23," an intra-school history quiz promoting historical interest among primary to upper section students. The committee meticulously organized papers for Grades 4-11, emphasizing continuous learning and healthy competition. This initiative aimed at inspiring historical exploration, integrating social media engagement, physical quizzes, and a grading system. The awarding ceremony celebrated participants, highlighting the Club's commitment to fostering historical awareness, academic excellence, and community among students.

Echoes of Antiquity

The Echoes of Antiquity website, launched by the Royal College History Club, offers an immersive exploration of Sri Lanka's heritage. Its innovative "Tree of Ages" visually organizes historical events, leading to dedicated web pages with animated videos, artwork, and detailed articles. Interactive quizzes at each section enhance engagement and understanding. This completed platform invites enthusiasts and students to delve into Sri Lankan history interactively, fostering an immersive and rewarding learning experience.

Historia'23

"Historia'23," a testament to Royal College History Club's enduring dedication, is an ongoing magazine exploring diverse historical themes like Ancient Egypt, Sri Lanka's Kingdoms, and Global Wars. Launched on October 20, 2023, it aims to engage students with attractive historical narratives, covering unknown facts, cultural diversity, beliefs, rituals, and technological advancements. Beyond dissemination, it orients students to historical study, global wars, and inquiry methods. Operated voluntarily and budget-free, "Historia'23" embodies the Club's passionate and committed historical odyssey.

Paramount'23

Paramount'23, a collaboration between Royal College's History Club and Radio Club, has concluded its multi-phase initiative. It began with a captivating video documentary about the school, followed by daily historical broadcasting sessions. The project's pinnacle was an electronic and physical magazine, delving deep into the school's rich history. Combining expertise from both clubs, Paramount'23 aimed to create a lasting impact by engaging students in an immersive exploration of Royal College's past, fostering enlightenment among its students.

RCHC Website

The Royal College History Club unveiled its crucial website, marking a pivotal moment. This platform boasts a chronological journey of the club, emphasizing recent projects and ongoing initiatives. Special sections spotlight current endeavors with detailed information and visuals. The interactive website facilitates direct communication with club management and simplifies membership through an online registration form, streamlining the joining process for interested individuals.

Weekly Facts

The Royal College History Club completed its year-long "Weekly Facts" project, launched on April 10, 2022. Focused on historical awareness, it highlighted significant events from Sri Lankan and world history, shared via social media and printed posters. Accurate and engaging content was key, disseminated on platforms such as WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook. The weekly unveiling encouraged examination and reflection on historical content, reinforcing the Club's legacy of nurturing a passion for history within the Royal College community.

Weekly Pop Quiz '23

The Weekly Pop Quiz '23, by the Royal College History Club in 2023, utilized social media for engaging historical questions. Beginning June 19, 2023, weekly quizzes with ten themed questions aimed to spark curiosity, enhance retention, and build a history enthusiast community. This project showcased the Club's dedication to making history a vital part of modern education, reflecting resilience and commitment.
